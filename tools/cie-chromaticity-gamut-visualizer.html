CIE 1931 Diagnostics

The CIE 1931 color space represents the first mathematical definition of human color vision, mapped by the International Commission on Illumination.

The curved boundary (spectral locus) illustrates purely monochromatic light across the visible spectrum (380nm to 780nm). Every color perceivable by the human eye exists within this horseshoe shape.

The triangular overlays denote gamuts—the physical limits of specific display hardware. Any coordinate outside a triangle cannot be accurately reproduced by that specific standard, leading to desaturation or clipping on the target device.
